About Me

{# #}

Shu Huang (sh2009@cam.ac.uk)

I am a PhD student at the Molecular Engineering group in the Cavendish Laboratory of the University of Cambridge, under the guidance of Professor Jacqueline Cole. I manage and maintain the Battery Data project, which focuses on leveraging text mining and Natural Language Processing (NLP) techniques to analyse the scientific literature on battery materials. Specifically, my work involves developing rule-based applications to extract large-scale data, as well as training and deploying state-of-the-art transformer-based language models. Through these efforts, I have contributed to four published research papers and have also created several open-source language models and packages, including BatteryBERT and BatteryDataExtractor.

Following the submission of my PhD thesis, I co-founded Cambridge alphaLex, a consultancy company specializing in natural language processing and generative AI. Our team consists of Cambridge University Alumni including NLP PhDs and cloud software experts. Our mission is to help business decision-makers understand the latest AI technologies and their potential impact. We provide expert guidance on implementing these technologies in your workflows, tailored to meet the specific needs of your business.
